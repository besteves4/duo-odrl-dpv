Ad-hoc Ontology providing additional necessary concepts for creating ODRL rules from DUO Concepts

Vocabulary

DUODRL provides terms based on the Data Use Ontology (DUO) to represent policies for health data sharing as ODRL policies. The Party concepts correspond to concepts that can be used to restrict the usage of certain data to certain entities. The Asset concepts correspond to concepts related to output assets of research results. The Action concepts correspond to concepts related to actions to be performed over data assets. The Left operand concepts correspond to concepts that can be used to restrict the usage of certain data to certain projects, diseases, population groups, age or gender and the Right operand concepts correspond to concepts that can be used to restrict the usage of certain data to certain purposes or geographic locations. The Template concepts correspond to query templates used to retrieve values that are not know at the time of the creation of the policy.

Parties

  • NonProfitOrganisation: Party is a non profit organisation.
  • ForProfitOrganisation: Party is a for profit organisation.

Assets

  • ResultsOfStudies: Output asset which is the result of the studies being exercised.

Actions

  • CollaborateWithStudyPI: Collaborate with the Primary Study Investigator.
  • ProvideEthicalApproval: Provide documentation of local IRB/ERB approval.
  • ReturnDerivedOrEnrichedData: Return derived or enriched data to the resource.

Left operands

  • Project: Restrict usage of health data to a certain project setup.
  • Disease: Restrict usage of health data to do research in a certain disease.
  • PopulationGroup: Restrict usage of health data to do research in a certain group.
  • Age: Restrict usage of health data to do research in a certain age group.
  • Gender: Restrict usage of health data to do research in a certain gender group.

Right operands

  • CC: Clinical use and care purpose.
  • DS: Disease specific research purpose.
  • GRU: General research use purpose.
  • GS: Genetic studies purpose.
  • GSG: Genotype studies purpose.
  • GSP: Phenotype studies purpose.
  • HMB: Health or medical or biomedical research purpose.
  • MDS: Methods development research purpose.
  • NCU: Non-commercial research purpose.
  • POA: Population origins or ancestry research purpose.
  • AncestoryResearch: Ancestry research purpose.
  • GenderCategoryResearch: Gender category research purpose.
  • AgeCategoryResearch: Age category research purpose.
  • PopulationGroupResearch: Population group research purpose.
  • ResearchControl: Research control research purpose.
  • DrugDevelopment: Biomedical research concerning drug development.
  • GeographicLocation: Geographic Location.

Operators

  • isNotA: Indicates that a given left operand is not an instance of the right operand of the constraint.

Templates

  • TemplateQuery: SPARQL query template used to retrieve values that are not know at the time of the creation of the policy.
  • sparqlExpression: Associates a template with the query to be executed.
  • TemplateDataset: Represents a placeholder template for dataset (asset).
  • TemplateDisease: Represents a placeholder template for disease (as an instance of MONDO_0000001).
  • TemplateStudyResultsPublicationDate: Represents a placeholder template for study results publication date.
  • TemplateTimeLimit: Represents a placeholder template for time limit.
  • TemplateUser: Represents a placeholder template for user.
  • TemplateProject: Represents a placeholder template for project.
  • TemplateInstitution: Represents a placeholder template for institution.
  • TemplateLocation: Represents a placeholder template for location.
  • TemplateResearch: Represents a placeholder template for research.
  • TemplateAgeCategory: Represents a placeholder template for age categories.
  • TemplateGender: Represents a placeholder template for gender.
  • TemplatePopulationGroup: Represents a placeholder template for population group.
